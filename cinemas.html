Iconic Cinemas Around the World

SM Cinema

★ ★ ★ Manila, Philippines

Why It's Famous

Largest cinema chain in the Philippines with over 300 branches nationwide, known for its IMAX and Director's Club premium experiences.

SM Cinema revolutionized movie-going in the Philippines by integrating theaters within shopping malls. Their premium offerings like IMAX and Director's Club with recliner seats have set new standards for comfort and viewing experience in the country.

IMAX Theaters Director's Club Nationwide Chain

CGV Cinemas

Seoul, South Korea

Fun Fact

Largest multiplex chain in South Korea, famous for its 4DX technology that adds motion, wind, and scent effects to movies.

CGV is Korea's leading cinema chain with innovative viewing experiences like 4DX, ScreenX (270-degree viewing), and private screening rooms. They're known for their high-tech approach to cinema and premium services that enhance the movie-watching experience.

4DX Technology ScreenX Premium Services

TCL Chinese Theatre

Hollywood, USA

Why It's Famous

Home to the most famous concrete forecourt in the world, featuring handprints and footprints of Hollywood legends since 1927.

This historic cinema opened in 1927 and has been the site of numerous movie premieres, including the first Star Wars film. Its iconic Chinese-inspired architecture makes it one of the most photographed landmarks in Hollywood, attracting millions of visitors annually.

Historic Movie Premieres Tourist Attraction

Toho Cinemas

Tokyo, Japan

Fun Fact

Japan's largest cinema chain, famous for its Godzilla-themed cinema in Shinjuku with a massive Godzilla head on the building.

Toho Cinemas is Japan's premier movie theater chain, known for its high-quality screens and sound systems. The Shinjuku location is particularly famous for the giant Godzilla head that looms over the building, becoming an iconic Tokyo landmark and popular photo spot for tourists.
